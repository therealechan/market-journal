### II. Oil-Equity Correlation Breakdown — The Week's Most Important New Observation

The traditional logic: high oil → inflation pressure → Fed can't cut → growth stocks suffer.

But Friday (4/4) showed an anomaly:
- WTI futures remained **above $110**
- Equities did **not** sell off further — certain sectors even rallied strongly

**Two interpretations of this divergence:**

- **Constructive read (base case):** Markets are beginning to price in ceasefire expectations. If US-Iran negotiations produce real progress, oil will drop sharply, and the market is effectively front-running that. Oil high but equities stable = the pricing logic is shifting.

- **Cautious read (risk case):** This may be a one-off technical divergence rather than a structural shift. If ceasefire talks collapse, sustained high oil prices could force equities to play catch-up to the downside.

**Bottom line: The divergence is a positive signal, but at least 2-3 weeks of sustained observation is needed to confirm a structural shift.**

### V. The Key Risk — Strait of Hormuz

The week's primary macro tail risk remains concentrated in the Iran situation:

- US-Iran ceasefire talks ongoing, but no substantive breakthrough
- Strait of Hormuz traffic is the core variable for oil prices
- Trump signaled willingness to stop the war "even if the Strait hasn't fully reopened" — suggesting oil may have room to fall ahead of a formal ceasefire

**If ceasefire talks produce real progress → Oil falls sharply → Market sentiment improves dramatically**
